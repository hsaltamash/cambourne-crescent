# Great Common Farm - Cambourne Crescent

A Beacon of Faith, Community, and Service

Cambourne is a growing and vibrant community, rich in diversity, potential,

and shared values.

As our town continues to grow, so does the need for warm, welcoming spaces

where people can come together, connect, learn, celebrate,

and support one another.

To help meet these community needs, we have taken an important first step

by purchasing land here in Cambourne.

This marks the beginning of a long-term vision,

a project dedicated to creating a

welcome, inclusive,

and sustainable space for the community.

This will be more than just a building.

It is a shared dream to create a welcoming sanctuary

and a vibrant hub that serves the diverse needs of our growing community.

A calm oasis for reflection, learning, and meaningful connection,

open to everyone in Cambourne and beyond, fostering peace and understanding.

We believe that successful community projects are built

with the community, not just for the community.

Over the coming months, we will be engaging with residents, schools,

neighbours, councillors, and many others to listen to your hopes,

ideas, and aspirations for this shared space.

Let’s imagine, together, what we can build for the future of Cambourne.

Our Vision: A Multi-Functional Space for All

Prayer Facilities

A serene space for daily and congregational prayers, accommodating worshippers. Designed for tranquility and spiritual reflection.

Community and Event Hall

A versatile space for community gatherings, cultural events, workshops, and social activities, fostering unity and engagement across all backgrounds.

Educational Classrooms

Dedicated classrooms for diverse educational programs including STEM, Arabic language, alongside youth clubs and skills development workshops.

Community Coffee Shop

A welcoming coffee shop with indoor/outdoor seating, serving as a social hub, potential food bank, and meeting space for the wider community.

Open Garden & Allotment

A tranquil green space for relaxation, social interaction, and community gardening. Plans include growing produce for a community soup kitchen and exploring beekeeping.

Ample & Eco-Friendly Parking

Thoughtfully designed parking to minimise environmental impact and ease traffic congestion, including support for The Vine School pick-ups/drop-offs.
