# Hajj :: A Journey Beyond Footsteps - Dr Sohail - Cambourne Crescent

Dr. Sohail Ejaz, from the University of Cambridge, shares his experince from his recent Hajj journey:

🕋 A Journey Beyond Footsteps

As soon as I received the confirmation that my Hajj application had been accepted, it changed my inside with the blessed feeling that Allah has accepted me as His guest. The months leading up to the pilgrimage became a whirlwind of logistical planning—vaccinations, travel bookings, packing—but there was also something deeper happening- cleansing of the heart and preparing to spiritually stand in front of ALLAH’s house in Makkah and before the Holy Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in Madinah. I began preparing not just my suitcase, but my soul.

I spent hours immersed in learning the rites of Hajj, reading about the struggles of Prophet Ibrahim (AS), the perseverance of Hajar, and the lessons hidden in every ritual. I made amends with people I had unknowingly hurt, paid off debts, and wrote a will—not out of fear, but of clarity.

✈️ Departing with Intention

When the plane lifted off, I realized I was carrying with me more than my luggage. I had brought along my ego, my regrets, my unanswered questions. But I also carried hope. Hope that this journey would peel away the layers of noise and confusion life had piled on me.

🏜 The Days in Mina, Arafat, and Muzdalifah

In the tents of Mina, surrounded by people of every race and color, I felt true equality for the first time. At Arafat, I stood with my arms raised to the heavens, sobbing—not just from emotion, but from release. It felt like everything I had bottled up for years flowed freely in those sacred hours.

Sleeping under the stars in Muzdalifah, I remembered how small I was in the grand design of the universe, yet somehow still seen by my Creator.

🕋 Circling the Kaaba, Changing My Orbit

Tawaf—the act of circling the Kaaba—was where I felt a spiritual gravity I’d never known. Each step scrubbed something away: my doubts, my pride, the false identities I’d built to fit into the world. I was no longer just orbiting the Kaaba; I was realigning my inner compass.

🌙 Returning Different

When I returned home, people asked me how my trip was, expecting travel stories or heat complaints. But my answers were quieter. I had made a private vow: to pray Fajr on time no matter what, to donate more in secret than in show, to listen deeply—especially to those who could offer me nothing, forgiving the people – not because those who wronged me deserved it, but because I deserved peace, reduced my social media use, volunteering, and spoke more kindly to elders and youngers.

I now believe that I have been given a clean slate after my hajj journey and I wish to write something beautiful on it.

May ALLAH accept my Hajj & prayers and support me in becoming a better person. Additionally, I would also like to pray that may ALLAH accept you for this noble worship. Aameen.
