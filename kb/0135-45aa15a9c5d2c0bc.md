What is Ramadan?

Ramadan is the fourth of the five pillars of Islam.

The actual night that the Qur'an was revealed is a night known as Lailut ul-Qadr ('The Night of Power').

"The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said: 'There has come to you Ramadan, a blessed month, which Allah, the Mighty and Sublime, has enjoined you to fast. In it the gates of heavens are opened and the gates of Hell are closed, and every devil is chained up. In it Allah has a night which is better than a thousand months; whoever is deprived of its goodness is indeed deprived."'

[Sunan an-Nasa'i 2106]

Taraweeh Attendees - Expectation

Entry & Access

- Meeting Room: Strictly no entry unless booked.

Fire Safety & Alarms

- Fire Doors (on both sides of the HUB) must remain closed at all times.

Cleanliness & Hygiene

- Ensure the venue is left clean after prayers.

- Perform Wudu at home before arriving.

Water Arrangements

- Please bring your own water bottles.

- Parents are requested to bring water for children to prevent spills.

Shoe Management

Volunteer Support

- Volunteers will be present in the corridor and near the bathrooms.

- Attendees should not assemble outside the HUB, especially after prayers.

- Sister volunteers will maintain decorum in the sisters' area and ensure silence before prayers.

- If helping with carpet storage, ensure they are neatly stacked in the storeroom.

Children’s Supervision

- Children must be supervised at all times.

- Volunteers will request parental intervention if needed.

- Children should pray next to parents or stay home if not praying. The venue is not a playground.

Taraweeh Etiquette

- Maintain silence during Taraweeh to ensure a peaceful prayer environment.

Taraweeh Volunteers (5 per night) — Duties

Ask others for help if needed.

1) Start Volunteer (Entrance & Setup)

- Arrive early, help set up the prayer area.

- No gathering in the entrance/lobby before opening.

- Ask early arrivals to wait in cars until entry time.

- Remind: wudu at home (toilets restricted), bring water + prayer mat.

- Invite early arrivals to help set up.

⸻

2) Corridor Volunteer (Safety & Movement)

- Stay in corridor (pray, then return during break).

- Children only leave with a parent.

- Keep corridors clear/quiet; guide people smoothly.

- Do not open fire doors (alarm risk).

- Reduce unnecessary movement during ṣalah.

⸻

3) Corridor Volunteer (Toilets & Conduct)

- Stay in corridor (pray, then return during break).

- Toilets: avoid if possible; keep tidy if used.

- Parents to supervise children closely.

- Stop misuse (water play/pranks); leave toilets clean.

- Maintain adab: low voices, no loitering, follow instructions.

⸻

4) End Volunteer (Pack Down & Lock Up)

- Lead tidy-up: roll/return mats, clear hall.

- Final walk-through (hall + toilets); check lost items.

- Quiet exit: no hanging around outside; leave calmly.

- Turn off all lights.

- Padlock doors (key in key box outside).

⸻

5) Misc Volunteer (Parking & Support)

- Photos at start + end (hall, bins, toilets) for clealiness records.

- Parking: no main road parking; direct to Hub/Morrisons/Blue Space.

- Help with mats, latecomers, questions; support other volunteers.

- Escalate serious issues (alarms/safety/disputes).

Reminder: Good manners and care of the venue help keep Taraweeh running for everyone.
