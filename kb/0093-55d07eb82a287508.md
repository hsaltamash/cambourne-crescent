# Crescent School Plans for Growth and Enhanced Learning - Cambourne Crescent

On Thursday, November 7, Crescent School held a productive three hour long meeting to discuss its vision and ongoing improvements aimed at becoming an exceptional educational institution. The meeting brought together teachers and administrators to review key areas for development, followed by a dinner to show appreciation for the hard work and dedication of the school’s 25 teachers and assistants. As enrollment has grown to over 150 students, the school continues to benefit from its recent move to Sunday sessions, which has boosted attendance and focus among students. However, the administration emphasized the need for additional help to maintain the school’s growth and positive impact.

Aftab emphasized that Cambourne Crescent was founded solely to ensure quality education for young people.

Among the highlights of the meeting, Ms. Hinar shared updates on curriculum enhancements, focusing on aligning educational materials with the school’s Islamic values and Arabic education. New resources and lesson plans are in development to enrich students’ learning experiences and provide teachers with better tools. To address parents’ requests, the school also plans to share termly learning objectives, making it easier for families to support their children’s progress at home.

Sheikh Ali Omar emphased the importance of education in instilling good manners and strong morals in our children, and highlighted the vital role that teachers play in this process.

Abdellatif proposed measures to strengthen parental involvement through regular workshops and open days, which would allow parents to understand the curriculum and explore ways to assist their children’s learning. A digital portal was also dicussed to enable parents to track homework, monitor student progress, and receive updates on school events, creating a more streamlined communication system.

Ms. Sobia presented various school management improvements to enhance daily operations. Her proposals included better role definitions for volunteers, updates to the attendance policy, and more efficient procedures for teacher-parent communication. She emphasized the need for a clean, organized learning environment, with volunteer teams assisting in classroom cleaning before leaving each day.

Ms. Bushra added suggestions aimed at improving accountability, such as ID badges for students, punctuality records, and improved monitoring of homework distribution. She outlined plans to track homework release times to ensure students and parents receive it in a timely manner, making it easier for families to manage assignments alongside other commitments.

Ms. Lobna reviewed the school’s safeguarding policies, underscoring the importance of legal compliance, including DBS checks for all staff and volunteers. These updates ensure that students are learning in a safe and supportive environment, aligned with best practices in education.

All our school teachers hold DBS certifications.

We recognize that a four-hour weekend session may feel lengthy for some students; however, for the majority of students—and especially for our teachers—this schedule provides the best opportunity for quality education and meaningful learning time.

We kindly request all parents to be punctual with drop-offs and pick-ups, ensure their child completes homework, and offer their support. Parental involvement is essential to our students’ success.

Throughout the meeting, it was evident that Crescent School has made significant strides, yet a need remains for more community involvement. Volunteers from the parent and local community are encouraged to lend a hand in various roles, from helping with classroom maintenance to supporting school events. Those interested in volunteering are invited to contact the school administration to make a difference in students’ lives.

We are in need of additional teachers. If you are interested in teaching and contributing to our school, please reach out to us.

Crescent School remains committed to providing high-quality education for its students. With continued support from parents, dedicated teachers, and active community participation, the school is well-positioned to achieve new milestones in Islamic and Arabic learning, benefiting current students and future generations.
