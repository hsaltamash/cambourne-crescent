# My Hajj Journey - Wasi - Cambourne Crescent

Wasi writes about his Hajj Journey.

Firstly I am thankful to Allah SWT, who gave me the opportunity to visit his sacred house and complete my Hajj journey easily. From my Hajj experience, I learned that there are three key aspects to prepare for:

- Financial Preparation

- This part is manageable with planning. Start saving early—set aside a small amount each month.

- When booking through Nusuk, it’s wise to have extra funds as a buffer. Packages can fill up or change, so plan for 2 to 4 backup options to avoid disappointment.

- Physical Preparation

- Hajj requires stamina. Prepare your body with daily walks and light exercise.

- Avoid overtraining—no need for intense workouts or heavy lifting. Focus on building steady endurance.

- Spiritual Preparation (Most Important)

- Hajj is a deeply spiritual journey, and preparing your heart and soul in advance is essential—just like studying before an exam.

- Here’s what helped me spiritually:

- Daily Dhikr (Remembrance of Allah)

* Practice doing dhikr during everyday activities like walking, shopping, or chores.

* It becomes second nature and helps you stay spiritually connected during Hajj. - Morning and Evening Azkar

* Build a habit of reciting morning and evening Azkar now. It will help keep your heart engaged during the journey. - Learn About Hajj

* Study the Manasik of Hajj—know the rituals, steps, and the meaning behind them. This knowledge brings confidence and deeper connection. - Prepare for the Day of Arafah

* This is the most significant day of Hajj. As the Prophet ﷺ said: “Hajj is Arafah.”

* Prepare a personalized list of Duas in advance to make the most of this day.

Suggested categories of Duas- Duas for yourself (Deen, dunya, health, job, etc.)

- Family

- Spouse

- The Ummah

- Neighbours

- Community

- Anyone who asked for Duas

- Daily Dhikr (Remembrance of Allah)

Final Thought When you prepare spiritually, Allah’s help and blessings will come many times over, making your Hajj experience smoother and more meaningful.
